## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is creatine?
Creatine is a naturally occurring compound stored in muscles that helps produce ATP, the body's primary energy source for short, high-intensity activities such as weight training and sprinting. Supplementing with creatine helps replenish these stores and supports exercise performance.

### Is creatine safe for daily use?
Yes. Creatine is generally considered safe for healthy adults when consumed at the recommended serving size. Staying adequately hydrated while using creatine is recommended.

### Does creatine affect kidney health?
Current research has not shown creatine supplementation to harm healthy kidneys when used as directed. Individuals with kidney disease or other medical conditions should consult a healthcare professional before using creatine.

### What are the benefits of creatine?
Regular creatine supplementation may help:
- Increase strength and power output
- Improve high-intensity exercise performance
- Support muscle growth
- Enhance post-workout recovery
- Support cognitive performance
- Contribute to overall training capacity

### Should I use a loading phase?
A loading phase is optional. Some people choose to take multiple servings per day for the first 5–7 days to increase muscle creatine stores more quickly, followed by a maintenance dose. Others simply take the regular daily serving from the beginning and reach similar levels over time.

### Can beginners take creatine?
Yes. Creatine is suitable for both beginners and experienced athletes who want to improve strength, power, and training performance when combined with regular exercise and a balanced diet.

### Should I consult a healthcare professional before taking creatine?
If you have a medical condition, are pregnant or breastfeeding, are taking prescription medications, or have concerns about whether creatine is appropriate for you, consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting supplementation.
